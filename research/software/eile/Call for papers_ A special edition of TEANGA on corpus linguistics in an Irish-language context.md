Researchers are invited to submit unpublished original papers (up to 6,000 words) and project reports (up to 2,000 words) to a special bilingual edition of **TEANGA**, the Journal of the Irish Association for Applied Linguistics, which will focus on corpus linguistics in the context of the Irish language. **This will be the first publication to focus entirely on the field of corpus linguistics in the context of the Irish language** and it is hoped that it will become a point of reference in a field that has seen a flurry of activity in recent years.

TEANGA is published as an open-access, bilingual academic journal and is indexed in Scopus. The editors of this special edition are Gearóid Ó Cleircín, Úna Bhreathnach and Micheál Ó Meachair.

The deadline for submissions is 30 June 2026. Each article will be double-blindly peer-reviewed before the editors make the final assessment of the work. Authors will be notified of the outcome of the peer review process by **30 September 2026**. Submissions should be made at journal.iraal.ie where detailed instructions in relation to the format of submissions are available. The special edition is scheduled to be published in early 2027.

Academic papers and project reports that address methodologies, tools and applications of corpus linguistics in an Irish-language context are welcome. Submissions will be accepted in Irish or English.

Below is a sample of the topics that will be welcomed by the editors:

**Methodologies and tools**

- Corpus design and compilation
- Collection and processing of corpus data
- Analytical techniques
- Critical evaluation of existing tools, techniques and theories
- Possibilities of artificial intelligence
- Corpus data for the training of large language models (LLMs)

**Corpus-based research**

- Phonology, morphology and dialectology
- Lexicography and terminology
- Language acquisition, teaching and assessment
- Stylistics and literary studies
- Media studies, historical texts, educational material, and other specific genres
- Phraseology
- Discourse analysis
- Translation studies
- Sociolinguistics and language change
- Genres, domains and specialized languages

**Important dates**

30 June 2026: Complete articles submitted to [journal.iraal.ie](https://journal.iraal.ie/index.php/teanga/about/submissions)

30 September 2026: Decisions on acceptance or rejection to be sent to authors; including peer feedback.

31 October 2026: Revised versions to be returned to editors.

Spring 2027: Publication of the special edition
