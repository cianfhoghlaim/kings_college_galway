# Celtic Language Teacher Supply Crisis

## Overview

A critical shortage of qualified teachers capable of teaching through Celtic languages threatens the expansion and sustainability of language-medium education across all jurisdictions. This document analyzes the crisis and compares mitigation strategies.

---

## 1. Crisis Summary

### 1.1 Severity by Jurisdiction

| Jurisdiction | Sector | Severity | Key Statistic |
|--------------|--------|----------|---------------|
| **Wales** | Secondary | Critical | 15% recruitment target met |
| **N. Ireland** | Post-Primary | Critical | 50% specialist posts unfilled |
| **Rep. Ireland** | Primary | Severe | 43% schools have vacancies |
| **Scotland** | Secondary | Moderate | Distance learning reliance |
| **Isle of Man** | All | Limited | Single school |

### 1.2 Root Causes

| Factor | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **Linguistic barrier** | High proficiency requirement |
| **Pipeline failure** | ITE targets missed |
| **Resource burden** | Teachers create own materials |
| **Competition** | Other sectors offer better conditions |
| **Geography** | Rural posting challenges |
| **Retention** | Burnout and workload |

## 2. Wales - Welsh-Medium Teacher Supply

### 2.1 Current Workforce (2023/24)

| Level | Teachers (Welsh medium) | Target (2031) | Gap |
|-------|-------------------------|---------------|-----|
| **Primary** | 2,792 | 3,900 | -1,108 |
| **Secondary** | 2,029 | 3,200 | -1,171 |
| **Total** | 4,821 | 7,100 | -2,279 |

### 2.2 ITE Recruitment Performance

| Subject | Target | Achieved | % Met |
|---------|--------|----------|-------|
| **Welsh (subject)** | - | 15% | Critical |
| **Secondary (all)** | - | 62% | Poor |
| **Welsh-medium ITE** | 30% | <20% | Below target |

### 2.3 Welsh in Education Workforce Plan

**Initiatives:**
1. Sabbatical scheme for upskilling
2. Welsh language improvement courses
3. Subject conversion programs
4. Financial incentives (limited)

### 2.4 Challenges

- NASUWT survey: 43% supply teachers report problems finding work despite shortages
- Geographic mismatch between supply and demand
- Agency fees create barriers
- Competition from English-medium sector

## 4. Republic of Ireland - Teacher Supply

### 4.1 Vacancy Data (November 2025)

| School Type | Long-term Vacancies | Schools Affected |
|-------------|---------------------|------------------|
| **Gaelscoileanna** | 43% | ~66 schools |
| **English-medium** | 10% | ~300 schools |
| **Special schools** | 50%+ | ~50 schools |
| **DEIS schools** | Higher than average | - |

### 4.2 Requirements

| Requirement | Standard |
|-------------|----------|
| Primary qualification | B.Ed or PME + SCG |
| Irish proficiency | SCG (Scrúdú le Cáilíocht sa Ghaeilge) |
| Gaeltacht schools | Additional Gaeltacht placement |
| Subject specialists | Degree + Irish fluency |

### 4.3 INTO Union Position

"Government indifference" to crisis:
- Unqualified substitutes filling gaps
- Immersion model compromised
- Disproportionate impact on IME/DEIS/Special

### 4.4 Mitigation Attempts

| Initiative | Description | Status |
|------------|-------------|--------|
| Graduate conversion | For other language speakers | Limited uptake |
| Recruitment campaigns | Marketing IME | Ongoing |
| Pay incentives | None specific | Requested |
| Visa support | International teachers | Bureaucratic barriers |

## 5. Scotland - GME Teacher Supply

### 5.1 Current Situation

| Challenge | Impact |
|-----------|--------|
| Secondary specialists | Acute shortage |
| Geography | Remote posting issues |
| Materials | Teacher-created reliance |

### 5.2 Mitigation Strategies

| Strategy | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| **e-Sgoil** | Online teaching platform |
| **Immersion courses** | Year-long fluency programs |
| **Bursaries** | Financial incentives |
| **Partnership** | Stòrlann resource support |

### 5.3 e-Sgoil Model

**Features:**
- Distance learning for secondary subjects
- Connects remote schools
- Specialist teachers shared across councils
- Video conferencing delivery

**Limitations:**
- Not true immersion environment
- Technical challenges in rural areas
- Limited subject coverage

## 9. Policy Recommendations

### 9.1 Universal Actions Needed

1. **Strategic shortage designation**
   - Financial incentives
   - Visa fast-track
   - Recruitment campaigns

2. **ITE reform**
   - Increase Celtic-medium places
   - Funded immersion year
   - Subject specialist focus

3. **Retention strategy**
   - Workload reduction
   - Resource investment
   - Career progression

4. **Resource investment**
   - Centralized material development
   - Digital platform expansion
   - Cross-jurisdictional sharing

### 9.2 Jurisdiction-Specific

| Jurisdiction | Priority Action |
|--------------|-----------------|
| **Wales** | Secondary recruitment crisis response |
| **N. Ireland** | Specialist teacher pipeline |
| **Rep. Ireland** | Vacancy intervention fund |
| **Scotland** | Expand beyond e-Sgoil model |
