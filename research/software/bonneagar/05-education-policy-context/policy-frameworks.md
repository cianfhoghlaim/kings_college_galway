# Celtic Language Education Policy Frameworks

## Overview

Each jurisdiction operates under distinct legislative and policy frameworks for Celtic language education. This document compares approaches, targets, and implementation mechanisms.

---

## 1. Wales: Cymraeg 2050

### 1.1 Policy Overview

| Property | Value |
|----------|-------|
| **Strategy Name** | Cymraeg 2050: A million Welsh speakers |
| **Launch Date** | 2017 |
| **Target** | 1 million Welsh speakers by 2050 |
| **Legal Status** | Statutory framework |

### 1.2 Key Targets

| Target | Current | 2050 Goal |
|--------|---------|-----------|
| Welsh speakers | ~900,000 | 1,000,000 |
| Daily Welsh use | ~150,000 | 500,000+ |
| WM education | 21% | 40% |
| Transmission rate | ~25% | 50%+ |

### 1.3 Education Pillars

1. **Increase Welsh-medium provision**
   - 40% of pupils in WM education by 2050
   - New schools in English-speaking areas

2. **Expand workforce**
   - Welsh in Education Workforce Plan
   - Sabbatical scheme for upskilling

3. **Improve transition**
   - Foundation Phase (3-7) immersion
   - KS2-KS3 continuity

### 1.4 Implementation Mechanisms

| Mechanism | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **WESPs** | Welsh in Education Strategic Plans (local authority) |
| **Targets** | Statutory pupil targets per LA |
| **Categorization** | School language categorization system |
| **Funding** | Welsh-medium teaching grants |

### 1.5 Challenges

- Workforce shortage (15% secondary recruitment)
- Static 21% market share
- Urban expansion barriers
- HE drop-off (from 30% to 21% studying in Welsh)

## 2. Scotland: Gaelic Language Plan

### 2.1 Policy Overview

| Property | Value |
|----------|-------|
| **Framework** | Gaelic Language (Scotland) Act 2005 |
| **Body** | Bòrd na Gàidhlig |
| **Model** | Demand-led provision |
| **Legal Status** | Statutory duty to "have regard to" |

### 2.2 Key Features

1. **Demand-led model**
   - Parents request GME provision
   - Local authority must assess demand
   - No automatic right to provision

2. **National Gaelic Language Plan**
   - Updated every 5 years
   - Sets priorities for public bodies

3. **Education Scotland support**
   - Curriculum for Excellence (Gàidhlig)
   - e-Sgoil (online provision)

### 2.3 Funding Mechanisms

| Mechanism | 2025/26 Amount |
|-----------|----------------|
| **Gaelic Specific Grant** | £4.55M |
| **Highland Council allocation** | £940K |
| **Stòrlann (resources)** | ~£400K |

### 2.4 Challenges

- "Postcode lottery" of provision
- No GME in many council areas
- Secondary specialist shortage
- Community language decline
- 81% of "Gaelic learners" not in immersion

## 3. Northern Ireland: Identity and Language Act 2022

### 3.1 Policy Overview

| Property | Value |
|----------|-------|
| **Legislation** | Identity and Language (NI) Act 2022 |
| **Status** | Statutory duty |
| **Body** | Comhairle na Gaelscolaíochta (CnaG) |
| **Trigger** | Community-driven establishment |

### 3.2 Key Provisions

1. **Official status**
   - Irish recognized as official language
   - Irish Language Commissioner created

2. **Education duty**
   - DE NI must "encourage and facilitate" IME
   - Statutory basis for sector

3. **Cultural rights**
   - Identity rights framework
   - Ulster Scots also recognized

### 3.3 Funding Context

| Year | CnaG Budget | Change |
|------|-------------|--------|
| 2019 | ~£1.2M | Baseline |
| 2024 | ~£1.0M | -12% real terms |

### 3.4 School Establishment Process

1. Community group identifies demand
2. CnaG provides support and guidance
3. Development Proposal submitted
4. DE NI assessment
5. Minister decision
6. Provisional school opens (often in temporary accommodation)

### 3.5 Challenges

- 16/21 new schools in temporary buildings
- Capital investment backlog
- High SEN prevalence (32% vs 21% average)
- Specialist teacher shortage
- Resource gap (teacher-created materials)

## 4. Republic of Ireland: Dual Framework

### 4.1 Gaeltacht Policy

| Property | Value |
|----------|-------|
| **Policy** | Policy on Gaeltacht Education 2017-2022 (extended) |
| **Scheme** | Gaeltacht School Recognition Scheme |
| **Body** | Údarás na Gaeltachta |

**Recognition Criteria:**
- Must operate fully through Irish
- Meet language planning criteria
- Community engagement

### 4.2 Policy for IME Outside Gaeltacht

| Property | Value |
|----------|-------|
| **Policy** | Policy for Irish-medium Education (2025) |
| **Launch** | Late 2025 |
| **Response** | Criticized for lacking targets |

**Key Issues:**
- No specific targets for new schools
- "Taskforce on Models of Supply" established
- "Oversupply" policy blocks expansion

### 4.3 20-Year Strategy for Irish Language

| Property | Value |
|----------|-------|
| **Strategy** | 20-Year Strategy for the Irish Language 2010-2030 |
| **Target** | 250,000 daily speakers |
| **Status** | Substantially behind target |

### 4.4 Patronage System

| Patron Type | Schools | Notes |
|-------------|---------|-------|
| **An Foras Pátrúnachta** | ~70 | Dedicated Irish-medium patron |
| **Catholic Church** | Majority | Gaeltacht schools |
| **Educate Together** | ~5 | Some bilingual models |

### 4.5 Challenges

- 13 counties with no IME post-primary
- 43% of Gaelscoileanna have vacancies
- Waiting lists at oversubscribed schools
- Teacher qualification requirements
- Gaeltacht sociolinguistic decline

## 5. Isle of Man: Language Strategy 2022-2032

### 5.1 Policy Overview

| Property | Value |
|----------|-------|
| **Strategy** | Manx Language Strategy 2022-2032 |
| **Body** | Culture Vannin |
| **Status** | Government commitment |

### 5.2 Key Targets

| Target | Current | 2032 Goal |
|--------|---------|-----------|
| Speakers | ~2,500 | 5,000 |
| School provision | 1 primary | Expand |
| Subject teaching | ~1,842 pupils | Increase |

### 5.3 Education Provisions

1. **Bunscoill Ghaelgagh**
   - Government maintained (since 2020)
   - Island-wide catchment
   - ~70 capacity

2. **Manx as subject**
   - Available in all schools
   - GCSE equivalent (Teisht Chadjin)
   - A-Level available

### 5.4 Unique Features

- Language revived from near-extinction
- UNESCO declared extinct (2009)
- ~170 fluent graduates from Bunscoill
- Adult learner community strong

## 7. Policy Recommendations (From Research)

### 7.1 Universal Needs

1. **Workforce strategy** - Treat Celtic teaching as shortage occupation
2. **Resource investment** - Reduce teacher material creation burden
3. **Capital planning** - Match infrastructure to demand
4. **Continuity support** - Primary to secondary transition

### 7.2 Jurisdiction-Specific

| Jurisdiction | Priority |
|--------------|----------|
| **Wales** | Secondary teacher recruitment |
| **Scotland** | Expand beyond 3 councils |
| **N. Ireland** | Permanent school buildings |
| **Rep. Ireland** | Lift new school restrictions |
| **Isle of Man** | Secondary immersion pathway |
